Rizal Childhood

José Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines, was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna, which was then a part of the Spanish colonial Philippines. He was the seventh child of Francisco Mercado Rizal and Teodora Alonso Realonda. Rizal's full name was José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda.

Rizal's childhood was marked by the influence of his parents, who were both highly educated and encouraged their children to pursue learning and excellence. His mother, Teodora Alonso, played a significant role in his early education, teaching him the basics of reading, writing, and arithmetic before he entered formal schooling.

At the age of three, Rizal showed exceptional intelligence and was able to read and write. His parents recognized his potential and provided him with opportunities for further education. He received his early education from private tutors and later attended schools in Binan, Laguna, and Manila.

In 1872, when Rizal was just eleven years old, he witnessed the execution of three Filipino priests who were accused of rebellion by the Spanish authorities. This event, known as the Cavite Mutiny, had a profound impact on Rizal and shaped his views on colonial oppression and the need for social and political reforms in the Philippines.

Throughout his childhood, Rizal showed a keen interest in literature, science, and the arts. He excelled in his studies and eventually pursued higher education in Manila, Spain, and Germany. Rizal's experiences during his formative years, including his exposure to Spanish colonialism and his education abroad, would later influence his writings and his role as a nationalist and reformist leader in the Philippines.
