Spain (1882-1885)

Rizal traveled to Spain in May 1882 to pursue higher education. He enrolled at the Universidad Central de Madrid (now Complutense University of Madrid) to study medicine and later transferred to the Universidad de Barcelona. During his time in Spain, Rizal immersed himself in the intellectual and cultural scene, engaged with fellow Filipino expatriates, and wrote articles for Filipino publications.

Europe (1885-1887)

While in Europe, Rizal undertook various travels to further his studies, broaden his cultural experiences, and observe societal conditions. He visited several European countries, including France, Germany, Belgium, and Switzerland. In Germany, he completed his ophthalmology training under Dr. Otto Becker in Heidelberg.

Japan (1888)

On his return journey to the Philippines from Europe, Rizal made a stopover in Japan. He spent a short time in the country, observing Japanese culture and society, and marveling at the discipline and industry of the Japanese people.

Hong Kong (1891)

Rizal briefly stayed in Hong Kong during his travels to escape political persecution in the Philippines. While in Hong Kong, he continued his advocacy for social reform and Philippine independence, engaging with fellow exiles and supporters of the reform movement.

Philippines (1892-1896)

Rizal returned to the Philippines in 1892 but was immediately arrested and exiled to Dapitan, Mindanao, by the Spanish authorities due to his involvement in the reform movement. Despite his exile, Rizal continued his intellectual pursuits, establishing a school, practicing medicine, and engaging in various scientific and agricultural projects.
