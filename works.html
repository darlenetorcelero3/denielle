Literary Works
by
Jose Rizal

Jose Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines, was a prolific writer whose literary works played a significant role in inspiring the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule. Some of his most notable literary works include:





"Noli Me Tangere" (Touch Me Not)

Published in 1887, this novel is considered one of the most important works in Philippine literature. It exposes the injustices and abuses of Spanish colonial rule in the Philippines and criticizes the corruption and hypocrisy of the Spanish friars and government officials.

"El Filibusterismo" (The Reign of Greed)

Published in 1891, this novel is a sequel to "Noli Me Tangere" and continues the story of its protagonist, Crisostomo Ibarra, who returns with a new identity as Simoun. Like its predecessor, "El Filibusterismo" explores themes of social injustice and resistance against oppression.

"Mi Ultimo Adiós" (My Last Farewell)

A poem written by Rizal on the eve of his execution in 1896. It expresses his love for his country, his willingness to sacrifice his life for its freedom, and his hope for a better future for the Philippines.

"Filipinas dentro de cien años"
(The Philippines Within a Century)

An essay written by Rizal in 1889, where he envisions the future of the Philippines and the challenges it may face. He predicts the eventual independence of the Philippines and calls for unity and progress among its people.
